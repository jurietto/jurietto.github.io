Privacy Policy

Data Collection

This site collects minimal data. When you post or comment, we store:

  • Username (optional, defaults to "Anonymous")
  • Post/Comment text (what you write)
  • Images (if you upload attachments)
  • Timestamp (when you posted)
  • Hashtags (if you use them)

What I don't collect:

  • No advertising cookies or tracking pixels
  • No third-party analytics (Google Analytics, etc.)
  • No email addresses or contact information
  • No intentional profiling or behavioral tracking

What Firebase/Google may collect:

  • IP addresses (for Firebase security and abuse prevention)
  • Browser/device information (user agent strings)
  • Authentication tokens if you log in
  • Basic usage metrics for Firebase services

I don't control or have access to most of what Firebase collects. Google has their own privacy policies for Firebase services.

Data Storage

All posts and comments are stored in Google Firebase Firestore, a cloud database service. Data is publicly readableâ€”anyone can see your posts and comments. Your content is stored on Google's servers and subject to their infrastructure and policies.

External Services

YouTube videos: We display static JPEG thumbnails, not actual YouTube embeds. When you click a video, it opens YouTube in a new tab - nothing from YouTube loads on this site until you click. Google can only track you after you leave this site and visit YouTube.

Links: We strip tracking parameters (utm_*, fbclid, gclid) from user-shared links. Links open in a new tab with no referrer information.

GitHub Pages: This site is hosted on GitHub Pages. GitHub may collect visitor logs and technical information. See GitHub's Privacy Statement for details.

Security

This site uses:

  • Content Security Policy to prevent script injection
  • No-referrer policy to prevent referrer leaks
  • Permissions Policy to disable unnecessary browser features
  • Input validation to prevent XSS attacks
  • HTTPS/TLS encryption for all data in transit (via GitHub Pages)

Editing and Deleting Your Posts

You can edit or delete your own posts without creating an account. Here's how it works:

  • When you visit the site, a random ID is generated and saved in your browser's localStorage
  • This ID is attached to any posts or comments you make
  • You can edit/delete posts made from the same browser because your browser ID matches
  • If you clear your browser data, switch browsers, or use a different device, you lose the ability to edit/delete your posts
  • The ID includes a browser fingerprint (user agent, platform, language) to prevent simple ID theft
  • This is not a security feature - it's a convenience feature to let you fix typos or remove posts

Your Rights

This is a small personal project:

  • Posts and comments are public by default
  • I don't sell or share your data with third parties
  • If you lost access to edit/delete (cleared browser data, new device), contact me to remove content manually
  • No ads, no sponsorships, no affiliate marketing
  • I'm not a lawyer and this isn't legal advice - if you have serious privacy concerns, don't post personal information.

Changes to This Policy

I may update this privacy policy if things change. Check back occasionally. Last updated: February 12, 2026

The Bottom Line

I built this to be as privacy-respecting as reasonably possible while using free hosting and database services. Firebase/Google/GitHub collect some stuff I can't control. I don't monetize this site or use your data for anything besides running the forum.
